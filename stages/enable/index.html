    Enable¶

    Stage 4 of 5 • Leveraged Capability

    Core Question

    Can I make others successful?

    Overview¶

    The Enable stage marks the transition from personal capability to leveraged capability. Output shifts from individual work to developing others. Practitioners at this stage mentor, coach, teach, and lead—their success is measured by the success of others.

    Output¶

    Capable People — Developed team members, mentored practitioners, built leadership.

    Characteristics¶

    • Mentors and coaches others
    • Teaches effectively
    • Builds team capability
    • Multiplies impact through people
    • Creates psychological safety

    Framework Mappings¶

    Framework Corresponding Stage(s)
    Bloom's Taxonomy Create
    Dreyfus Model Master (extended)
    Shu-Ha-Ri Ri (離)
    Apprenticeship Lead, Teach
    Conscious Competence Reflective Mastery

    Beyond Classical Frameworks

    Most classical frameworks don't explicitly model this stage. Dreyfus stops at Expert. Bloom focuses on cognitive mastery, not leadership. Enable is where LEVER extends beyond traditional models.

    What "Enable" Looks Like¶

    Product Builder¶

    • Mentors junior builders
    • Creates learning opportunities for the team
    • Builds high-performing product teams
    • Establishes product practices

    Software Engineer¶

    • Sets technical direction for the team
    • Mentors multiple engineers
    • Leads technical initiatives
    • Runs knowledge-sharing sessions

    Evidence of Enabling¶

    • Team members grow visibly under their guidance
    • Creates repeatable coaching approaches
    • Team succeeds when they're absent
    • Others seek them out for mentorship
    • Builds succession for their own role

    The Mindset Shift¶

    Value Mindset Enable Mindset
    How can I deliver? How can I help others deliver?
    What's my output? What's my team's output?
    Am I succeeding? Are others succeeding?
    My skills Team capabilities

    The Enable Test

    You've truly enabled when your team succeeds without you.

    Forms of Enabling¶

    Enable happens through multiple channels:

    1:1 Mentoring¶

    Direct coaching and guidance for individuals.

    Teaching¶

    Structured knowledge transfer to groups.

    Leading¶

    Guiding teams toward outcomes.

    Culture Building¶

    Creating environments where others can grow.

    Common Pitfalls¶

    Doing vs Developing

    Taking over tasks instead of coaching others through them. "Let me just do it" undermines growth.

    Hoarding Knowledge

    Not sharing expertise, context, or decision-making. Enablers are generous with information.

    Micromanagement

    Over-controlling rather than empowering. Enable requires trust and tolerance for learning mistakes.

    Transition to Replicate¶

    A practitioner is ready to transition to Replicate when they:

    • Have developed multiple successful team members
    • See patterns in how capability develops
    • Recognize limits of 1:1 enabling
    • Want to scale impact beyond direct relationships

    Key Insight¶

    Enable is where impact starts to multiply—but it's bounded by human relationships. A great enabler might develop 10-20 people in their career. Replicate asks: can you create systems that multiply capability without your direct involvement?

    In the AI era, enabling becomes both easier (AI can augment learning) and more important (humans need to develop judgment that AI lacks).
