# Learn

**Stage 1 of 5** • Personal Capability

!!! abstract "Core Question"
    **What do I know?**

## Overview

The Learn stage is about acquiring knowledge through study, observation, and instruction. Practitioners build vocabulary, concepts, and mental models that form the foundation for all subsequent capability development.

## Output

**Knowledge** — Understanding of concepts, terminology, principles, and frameworks.

## Characteristics

- Studies concepts and terminology
- Follows instructions and tutorials
- Asks questions to understand
- Builds foundational mental models

## What "Learn" Looks Like

### Product Builder

- Completes relevant courses and certifications
- Studies successful products and case studies
- Learns technical fundamentals (coding, AI, tools)
- Understands business models and metrics

### Software Engineer

- Completes coding tutorials and courses
- Understands basic CS concepts
- Learns team tools and practices
- Studies documentation and codebases

## Evidence of Learning

- Can explain concepts in their own words
- Passes knowledge assessments
- Completes structured learning programs
- Demonstrates growing vocabulary

## Common Pitfalls

!!! warning "Tutorial Hell"
    Getting stuck in endless learning without moving to execution. Learning should be purposeful and time-boxed.

!!! warning "Passive Consumption"
    Watching videos or reading without active engagement. True learning requires practice and reflection.

## Transition to Execute

A practitioner is ready to transition to [Execute](execute.md) when they:

- Have sufficient foundational knowledge
- Can follow instructions to complete tasks
- Understand the domain vocabulary
- Are ready to apply knowledge with guidance

## Key Insight

> Learning is necessary but not sufficient. The goal is not to accumulate knowledge but to build a foundation for **doing**.

In the AI era, learning can be accelerated through AI assistants that provide just-in-time knowledge. But deep understanding still requires active engagement and practice.
